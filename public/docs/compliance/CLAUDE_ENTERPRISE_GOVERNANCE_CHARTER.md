# Claude Enterprise Deployment Governance Charter

**Version:** 1.0 | **Effective Date:** December 2025 | **Review Cycle:** Quarterly

---

## Purpose & Scope

This charter establishes the governance framework for deploying and operating Claude AI within the enterprise environment. It defines roles, responsibilities, decision-making authority, and operational cadence to ensure secure, compliant, and effective AI deployment.

---

## Governance Structure

### 1. Executive Sponsor

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Provide strategic direction and alignment with enterprise objectives
- Approve budget allocations and resource commitments for Claude deployment
- Resolve escalated decisions and cross-functional conflicts
- Champion AI adoption across the organization
- Review quarterly performance metrics and ROI assessment

**Decision Authority:**
- Final approval on enterprise-wide Claude deployment strategy
- Budget approval over $50K
- Strategic vendor relationships and contract terms
- Business case approval for new Claude use cases

**Meeting Cadence:**
- Monthly: Governance steering committee (60 min)
- Quarterly: Executive business review (90 min)
- Ad-hoc: Escalation decisions (as needed)

---

### 2. Security Lead

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Define and enforce security controls for Claude deployment
- Conduct security assessments and penetration testing
- Monitor threat landscape and implement security patches
- Manage incident response for AI-related security events
- Approve technical security architectures and data protection measures

**Decision Authority:**
- Approve/reject deployments based on security posture
- Mandate security controls and encryption requirements
- Issue security waivers for specific use cases (with documentation)
- Halt operations in case of security incidents

**Meeting Cadence:**
- Weekly: Security operations review (30 min)
- Bi-weekly: Cross-functional security sync with Technical Lead (45 min)
- Monthly: Governance steering committee (60 min)
- Ad-hoc: Security incident response (as needed)

---

### 3. Compliance Lead

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Ensure Claude deployment meets regulatory requirements (SOC 2, GDPR, HIPAA, etc.)
- Manage vendor risk assessments and data processing agreements
- Maintain audit trails and compliance documentation
- Coordinate with legal on data privacy and AI ethics policies
- Conduct compliance reviews before major releases

**Decision Authority:**
- Approve/reject use cases based on regulatory compliance
- Define data handling and retention policies
- Mandate compliance controls and audit requirements
- Request business process changes to meet compliance standards

**Meeting Cadence:**
- Bi-weekly: Compliance review with Security and Technical Leads (45 min)
- Monthly: Governance steering committee (60 min)
- Quarterly: Regulatory assessment and audit preparation (90 min)
- Ad-hoc: Regulatory inquiries and audit support (as needed)

---

### 4. Technical Lead

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Architect and implement Claude integration with enterprise systems
- Manage technical operations, performance, and availability (SLA/SLO)
- Lead prompt engineering standards and model optimization
- Coordinate deployment pipelines and release management
- Provide technical escalation support and troubleshooting

**Decision Authority:**
- Approve technical architecture and integration patterns
- Define API usage policies and rate limits
- Select deployment models (cloud, on-premise, hybrid)
- Approve technical changes and system configurations

**Meeting Cadence:**
- Daily: Stand-up with engineering team (15 min)
- Bi-weekly: Cross-functional security sync with Security Lead (45 min)
- Bi-weekly: Technical planning with Business Lead (45 min)
- Monthly: Governance steering committee (60 min)

---

### 5. Business Lead

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Define business requirements and use case prioritization
- Measure business value, adoption metrics, and user satisfaction
- Manage change management and user training programs
- Coordinate with business units on Claude deployment roadmap
- Track ROI and communicate business outcomes to Executive Sponsor

**Decision Authority:**
- Prioritize use cases and feature requests
- Approve user access and role-based permissions
- Define success metrics and KPIs for Claude adoption
- Authorize pilot programs and phased rollouts

**Meeting Cadence:**
- Weekly: Business operations and user feedback review (45 min)
- Bi-weekly: Technical planning with Technical Lead (45 min)
- Monthly: Governance steering committee (60 min)
- Quarterly: Executive business review with Executive Sponsor (90 min)

---

## Governance Meetings

### Monthly Governance Steering Committee (60 min)
**Attendees:** All five governance leads  
**Agenda:** Deployment status, risk review, compliance updates, budget review, escalations

### Quarterly Executive Business Review (90 min)
**Attendees:** Executive Sponsor, Business Lead, + optional governance leads  
**Agenda:** Strategic alignment, ROI analysis, roadmap review, resource planning

---

## Escalation Path

**Level 1:** Technical/Business Lead (operational decisions)  
**Level 2:** Security/Compliance Lead (risk and compliance decisions)  
**Level 3:** Executive Sponsor (strategic and budget decisions)

**Escalation Criteria:** Budget overruns, compliance violations, security incidents, strategic misalignment, cross-functional disputes

---

## Amendment Process

This charter may be amended with approval from the Executive Sponsor and majority consensus from Security, Compliance, Technical, and Business Leads. All amendments require documentation and version control.
